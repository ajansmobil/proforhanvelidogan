What are the types of varicose veins?

Varicose veins are classified by clinical and anatomical features: telangiectasia (spider veins), reticular varicose veins, truncal varicose veins (saphenous and branches), and perforator vein insufficiency. Assoc. Prof. Orhan Veli Doğan is experienced in vascular and endovascular interventions (EVAR, TEVAR, carotid stenting, peripheral atherectomy and angioplasty). Correct identification of type and stage-appropriate treatment is offered.

Treatment options vary by type: sclerotherapy or laser for telangiectasia and reticular veins; endovenous ablation or surgery for truncal varicose veins; SEPS or other methods for perforator insufficiency. Diagnosis is by Doppler ultrasound and clinical examination.
