What is Inheritance?

Inheritance is the transfer of a person's assets, rights, and debts to others upon their death. This process takes place within the framework of legal regulations such as the Turkish Code of Obligations and the Inheritance Law. While inheritance is usually based on kinship, different persons may also be heirs in some cases, depending on a will or legal conditions.

What are the Conditions for Becoming an Heir?

For a person to be an heir, they must first have a kinship with the deceased or be designated by a will. The right to inheritance is generally evaluated in the following tiers:

  • 1st Degree Heirs: Children, spouse (if alive)
  • 2nd Degree Heirs: Grandchildren, mother, father, spouse (if alive)
  • 3rd Degree Heirs: Great-grandparents, siblings, spouse (if alive)

The inheritance right of individuals in these tiers may vary depending on the amount of assets left by the deceased and the status of other heirs. Furthermore, the right to inheritance may be lost in some cases due to wrongful acts or gross negligence.

What are the Types of Inheritance?

Inheritance is generally divided into two main types:

  • Statutory Inheritance: This is the type of inheritance that occurs based on kinship, in accordance with the provisions of the law. The degrees mentioned above constitute statutory inheritance.
  • Inheritance by Will: This is the transfer of assets to specific individuals or institutions by the will of the deceased. A will can take precedence over the rules of statutory inheritance, but certain legal limitations exist.

How Does the Inheritance Process Work?

To initiate the inheritance process, the death of the deceased must be officially documented and the heirs must be determined. Heirs can file a lawsuit for the division of the estate through an attorney or directly to the court. During this process, stages such as determination of the estate, payment of debts, and distribution of assets are completed.

Since the inheritance process can be complex, obtaining legal support from an inheritance attorney is important to protect your rights and complete the process more quickly. Especially if there are multiple heirs or the assets are significant, professional help is inevitable.
