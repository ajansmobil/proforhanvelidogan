What are varicose veins?

Varicose veins are a vascular condition characterised by dilation and swelling of veins and insufficiency of venous valves, leading to backward flow (reflux) of blood. They occur mainly in superficial leg veins and can cause pain, heaviness, swelling and skin changes.

Venous system and valves

Leg veins carry blood toward the heart. Valves allow blood to flow only upward; when valves fail, blood pools in the legs, increasing pressure and dilating veins. This is the main mechanism of varicose veins.

Who is affected?

Risk factors include older age, prolonged standing, inactivity, obesity, pregnancy, family history and sex (more common in women). Diagnosis is made by physical examination and Doppler ultrasound; treatment is planned according to stage and patient with lifestyle measures, compression, sclerotherapy, laser/radiofrequency or surgery.
