What Are Varicose Veins?

Varicose veins are a circulation problem where superficial veins enlarge and become twisted. They are mostly seen in the legs and may increase with prolonged standing, genetic tendency, pregnancy and low physical activity.

Early findings include heaviness, pain, evening swelling and visible dilated veins. Diagnosis is clarified by clinical examination and Doppler ultrasound.
