What Are Varicose Veins?
Varicose veins are dilation and insufficiency of veins. Assoc. Prof. Orhan Veli Doğan has experience in vascular and endovascular procedures (EVAR, TEVAR, peripheral angioplasty).
What Are the Types of Varicose Veins?
Types of varicose veins: telangiectasia, reticular, truncal, perforator insufficiency. Evaluated with vascular and endovascular treatment.
What Are the Stages of Varicose Veins?
Varicose vein stages are defined by CEAP classification. Stage-appropriate treatment with vascular and endovascular expertise.
What Causes Varicose Veins?
Causes: venous insufficiency, valve damage, prolonged standing, genetics. Diagnosis and treatment with vascular and endovascular approach.
What Are the Symptoms of Varicose Veins?
Symptoms: pain, swelling, heaviness, itching, skin changes. Evaluated with cardiovascular and endovascular expertise.
How Are Varicose Veins Diagnosed?
Diagnosis: physical exam, Doppler ultrasound, venous insufficiency assessment. Vascular and endovascular expertise.
How Is Varicose Vein Treatment Done?
Endovascular procedures for varicose veins: EVAR, TEVAR, carotid stenting, peripheral atherectomy and angioplasty. Complex vascular surgery experience.
