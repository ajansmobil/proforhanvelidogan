What are the Symptoms of Varicose Veins?

Varicose veins are a common condition caused by the enlargement and twisting of veins in the body, especially in the legs. In addition to aesthetic concerns, it can sometimes lead to health problems. Knowing the symptoms of varicose veins is important for early diagnosis and treatment. In this article, we will examine the symptoms of varicose veins in detail.

Most Common Symptoms of Varicose Veins

  • Swelling in the Legs: Swelling in the legs, especially during prolonged standing or sitting, may be one of the first signs of varicose veins.
  • Pain and Cramps: Leg pain, especially night cramps, is a frequently reported complaint. Pain usually increases with fatigue.
  • Skin Changes: Color changes in the skin in the area where varicose veins are present, redness, itching, or eczema-like rashes may occur.
  • Visible Veins: The appearance of blue or purple, raised veins under the skin is the most typical symptom of varicose veins. These veins usually spread upwards from below the knee on the legs.
  • Itching and Burning Sensation: Itching and burning sensation may occur on the skin due to varicose veins.

Less Common Symptoms of Varicose Veins

  • Pain in the Ankle and Feet: In severe cases of varicose veins, pain may also be felt in the ankle and feet.
  • Leg Ulcers: Prolonged varicose veins can cause open sores to form on the skin. These sores are often difficult to heal and carry a risk of infection.
  • Restless Legs Syndrome: Some varicose vein patients may experience symptoms of restless legs syndrome. This syndrome is characterized by an uncontrollable need to move the legs and a feeling of discomfort.
  • Risk of Deep Vein Thrombosis (DVT): Especially in situations where immobility is prolonged (e.g., long journeys), the risk of deep vein thrombosis increases in varicose vein patients. DVT is the formation of a blood clot in the leg and requires immediate medical attention.

Symptoms of varicose veins can vary from person to person. In some people, it only causes aesthetic concerns, while in others, it can lead to serious health problems. If you experience any of the above symptoms, it is important to consult a vascular surgeon and have the necessary examinations. Early diagnosis will help prevent the progression of varicose veins and evaluate treatment options.
