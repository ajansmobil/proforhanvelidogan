What are the Stages of Varicose Veins?

Varicose veins are a chronic circulatory disorder caused by the insufficiency of valves in the veins, making it difficult for blood to return from the legs to the heart. This condition can lead to various symptoms over time and can negatively impact quality of life as the stages progress. Understanding the stages of varicose veins is important for early diagnosis and selection of appropriate treatment methods.

Stages of Varicose Veins

Varicose veins often begin without symptoms and progress through different stages over time. These stages are generally classified as follows:

  • Stage 0: No Symptoms

In this stage, there are no visual signs or pain. However, weakness in the vein walls and valve insufficiency can be detected by imaging methods such as ultrasound. This stage is usually detected during routine examinations.

  • Stage 1: Spider Veins and Capillary Veins

Fine veins (spider veins or capillary veins) in red, blue, or purple begin to appear on the skin. These veins usually do not cause pain and are considered a cosmetic problem. However, this is one of the first signs of varicose veins.

  • Stage 2: Beginning of Small Varicose Veins

Small varicose veins (reticular veins) with a diameter of 1-3 mm begin to form under the skin. These veins are usually felt as a slight bump and can cause pain. Symptoms may worsen with prolonged standing or sitting.

  • Stage 3: Beginning of Large Varicose Veins

Varicose veins larger than 3 mm (venous trunk varicose veins) become visible. These veins are usually seen as bulging and winding under the skin. Pain, swelling, heaviness, and cramps are frequently observed. Symptoms may worsen with prolonged standing or sitting.

  • Stage 4: Skin Changes

Due to long-term varicose veins, skin color changes (hyperpigmentation or exfoliation), itching, eczema, and skin ulcers may begin to form. Treatment is more difficult in this stage and the risk of complications increases.
Skin changes are an indication that the varicose veins have been neglected.

  • Stage 5: Ulcers

In this most advanced stage, open wounds (venous ulcers) form on the skin. Ulcers are usually seen around the ankle and can be difficult to heal. Severe pain, risk of infection, and limited mobility are prominent features of this stage.

Early diagnosis and treatment are important to slow the progression of varicose vein stages and prevent complications. Your doctor can perform a physical examination and use imaging methods (ultrasound, venography) to determine your varicose vein stage. Treatment options include compression stockings, sclerotherapy, laser therapy, and surgical methods.
